Storm Shots — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 5, 2026

Storm Shots is a community weather app: you photograph storms, and your photos are pinned on a shared radar map so other users can see the same storm from multiple vantage points. This policy explains what the app collects and why.

Information we collect

  • Email address — used only to sign you in (passwordless one-time code) and to contact you about your account.
  • Display name (optional) — shown to other users next to your captures. If you don't set one, the part of your email before the @ is shown instead.
  • Photos you capture in the app — uploaded to our storage and visible to other signed-in users on the shared map and feed.
  • Location, compass heading, and time of each capture — recorded at the moment you take a photo so it can be pinned on the map pointing the way you were facing, together with the radar frame from that moment. These are shared with other users as part of the capture. Location is only read when you take a photo; there is no background location tracking.

What we don't do

  • No advertising, no analytics profiles, and we never sell your data.
  • We never read your photo library. If you choose "Save to Photos", the app only adds that one photo to your library.
  • The app does not record audio.

Service providers

Data is processed by these providers to run the app:

  • Supabase — hosts our sign-in, database, and photo storage.
  • Iowa Environmental Mesonet (Iowa State University) — serves the weather-radar imagery. Loading radar tiles discloses your IP address to their servers, as with any web content.
  • Google Maps (Android) / Apple Maps (iOS) — render the base map under their own privacy policies.

Retention & deletion

  • You can delete any of your captures in the app (open the capture → Delete). This removes the pin, its metadata, and the photo for everyone.
  • To delete your account and all associated data, email us at the address below and we will remove it within 30 days.

Children

Storm Shots is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect their data.

Changes

If this policy changes materially, we'll update this page and note the new date above.
